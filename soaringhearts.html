Ensol

Ensol is an isometric action-adventure game created in Unity where you uncover the mystery of the forest paradise that will do anything to not let you leave. This was my senior capstone that I created in a team of 8 people consisting of 4 programmers, 2 artists, 1 narrative designer, and 1 sound designer/composer. We utilized an agile style approach to developing the game and ended up completing the project in 2 quarters (5 months). My main focus for this project was programming all of the AI for the enemies, as well as implementing their animations. I created 3 enemies in total all with diverse behaviors that I implemented using behavior trees.

I worked on this game as a part of the Game Design and Art Collaboration club which runs a mock game studio every school year to create a game with a team of around 25 people. Coma Dream

Coma Dream is a 3D puzzle-platformer made in Unity based on the dreams one of my teammates had when they were in a medically induced coma. Our team had 7 people on it and we developed in the game in 2 weeks. Over those 2 weeks I programmed a 3D player controller using a finite state machine, cuboid planet physics, and AI for the runaway collectible keys.

Soaring Hearts

Soaring Hearts is a 2 player co-op puzzle-platformer made in Unity based on the prompt "Hidden Tutorials". Our team consisted of 9 people and we developed the game in 2 weeks. For this project I worked with a teammate to program a 2D player controller utilizing a finite state machine and implemented all of the player animations/sounds.

Soaring Hearts is a game about two kiwi birds on a journey to climb a mountain to make their flightless lives feel a little more free. The prompt "Hidden Tutorials" meant that we needed to teach players the mechanics our game through gameplay as opposed to walls of text. I primarily worked on the the programming for the kiwis, implementing all their sounds/animations and working with a teammate to create their movement controller.
