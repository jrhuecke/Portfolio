Ryan Hueckel

I am a game programmer who has recently graduated with a degree in Computer Science: Game Design from the University of California, Santa Cruz. Over my past 3 years of making games, I have collaborated in groups of 3-50 people to develop co-op 2D platformers, 3rd person shooters, isometric action-adventure games, and much more. I am always pushing myself to expand my skills and work on new and exciting projects that take me out of my comfort zone. From all of my projects, I am most experienced with programming character controllers, enemy AI, and user interfaces. Outside of game developement, I love playing tennis and am also a big fan of birds.

See some of the projects I have worked on below!

Ensol

About the Project:

Ensol is an isometric action-adventure game where you uncover the mystery of the forest paradise that will do anything to stop you from leaving. I worked in a team of 9 people to develop this game using Unity and we are releasing the game on Steam later this month!

My Role:

I acted as the lead programmer which involved assigning tasks and ensuring we met deadlines. My main focus for this project was coding all of the enemy AI which I implemented using behavior trees and context-based steering. Through working with our designers and character artist, I ended up creating 5 enemies in total, all with their own unique behaviors and attacks. Read more about our enemy creation process and my other contributions to the project here.

Dracomaton

About the Project:

Dracomaton is a fast-paced rogue-like all about swapping between different movesets called "forms" to fight off enemies. The game was created by a team of 14 people in Unity and is getting released on Steam later this summer.

My Role:

I joined this project as a programmer partway through its development which required lots of rapid learning of all parts of the game's code structure as I worked. During my time on the project I collaborated with artists and designers to implement content such as items which interacted with all parts of the game and therefore required lots of communication with the other programmers on the team.

Coma Dream

About the Project:

Coma Dream is a 3D puzzle-platformer based on the dreams one of my teammates had while in a medically induced coma. Our team consisted of 7 people and we developed the game in 2 weeks using Unity.

My Role:

I programmed a player controller using a finite state machine, cuboid planet physics, and behavior for the runaway collectible keys.

TacoCat

About the Project:

Soaring Hearts

About the Project:

Soaring Hearts is a 2 player co-op puzzle-platformer made in Unity based on the prompt "Hidden Tutorials". Our team consisted of 9 people and we developed the game in 2 weeks.

My Role:

For this project I collaborated with a teammate to create the 2D player controller utilizing a finite state machine and implemented all of the player animations/sounds.
