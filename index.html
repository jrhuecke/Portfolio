Ryan Hueckel

I am a game programmer currently finishing up my degree in Computer Science: Game Design at University of California: Santa Cruz. During my time in college I have collaborated in groups of 3-25 people to create games in many different styles/genres such as co-op 2D platformers, 3rd person shooters, and isometric action-adventure games. I am always trying to expand my skills with what I create and so far my favorite parts of games to program are character controllers, AI, and anything that makes the game feel more satisfying to play. Outside of game developement, I love playing tennis and am also a big fan of birds.

Ensol

Ensol is an isometric action-adventure game created in Unity where you uncover the mystery of the forest paradise that will do anything to not let you leave. This was my senior capstone which was made in 2 quarters (20 weeks) in a team of 9 students. As well as acting as the programming producer, my main focus for this project was writing the AI for all of the enemies which I implemented using behavior trees and context-based steering. Through working with our lead combat designer and character artist, I ended up creating 4 enemies in total, all with their own unique behavior and attacks. Read more about our enemy creation process and my other contributions to the project here.

Coma Dream

Coma Dream is a 3D puzzle-platformer made in Unity based on the dreams one of my teammates had when they were in a medically induced coma. Our team had 7 people on it and we developed in the game in 2 weeks. Throughout the project, I programmed a 3D player controller using a finite state machine, cuboid planet physics, and the behavior for the runaway collectible keys.

TacoCat

TacoCat is a 2D narrative driven game made up of truck driving and taco making minigames. I worked on this game as a part of the Game Design and Art Collaboration club which runs a mock studio ever year where students work together to develop a game. In the year I worked on TacoCat, there were around 50 people participating in the club spread out through different departments. My main contributions were to the taco making minigame which I did a lot of the customer programming for.

Soaring Hearts

Soaring Hearts is a 2 player co-op puzzle-platformer made in Unity based on the prompt "Hidden Tutorials". Our team consisted of 9 people and we developed the game in 2 weeks. For this project I collaborated with a teammate to create the 2D player controller utilizing a finite state machine and implemented all of the player animations/sounds.
