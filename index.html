Ryan Hueckel

I am a game programmer currently finishing up my degree in Computer Science: Game Design at University of California: Santa Cruz. In college I have collaborated in groups made up of 3-25 people to create games in many different styles/genres such as co-op 2D platformers, 3rd person shooters, and isometric action-adventure games. I love coding everything in games from player controllers to AI to UI, anything that can make the game more satisfying to play and most importantly, more fun.

Ensol

Ensol is an isometric action-adventure game created in Unity where you uncover the mystery of the forest paradise that will do anything to not let you leave. This was my senior capstone that I created in a team of 8 people consisting of 4 programmers, 2 artists, 1 narrative designer, and 1 sound designer/composer. We utilized an agile style approach to developing the game and ended up completing the project in 2 quarters (5 months). My main focus for this project was programming the enemies which I wrote the AI and implemented the animations for. I created X enemies in total all with diverse behaviors that I implemented using behavior trees.

TacoCat

TacoCat is a 2D story game consisting of truck driving and taco making minigames. I worked on this game as a part of the Game Design and Art Collaboration club which runs a mock game studio every school year to create a game with a team of around 25 people. My main contributions to the project were programming parts of the taco making minigame.

Soaring Hearts

Soaring Hearts is a 2 player co-op puzzle-platformer made in Unity based on the prompt "Hidden Tutorials". Our team consisted of 9 people and we developed the game in 2 weeks. For this project I worked with a teammate to program a 2D player controller utilizing a finite state machine and implemented all of the player animations/sounds.

Coma Dream

Coma Dream is a 3D puzzle-platformer made in Unity based on the dreams one of my teammates had when they were in a medically induced coma. Our team had 7 people on it and we developed in the game in 2 weeks. Over those 2 weeks I programmed a 3D player controller using a finite state machine, cuboid planet physics, and AI for the runaway collectible keys.
