Ryan Hueckel

I am a game programmer currently finishing up my degree in Computer Science: Game Design at University of California, Santa Cruz. Over my past 3 years of making games, I have collaborated in groups of 3-25 people to develop co-op 2D platformers, 3rd person shooters, isometric action-adventure games, and much more. I'm always pushing myself to expand my skills and work on new and exciting projects that take me out of my comfort zone. So far, my favorite parts of games to program are character controllers, enemy AI, and user interfaces. Outside of game developement, I love playing tennis and am also a big fan of birds.

See some of the projects I have worked on below!

Ensol

About the Project:

Ensol is an isometric action-adventure game where you uncover the mystery of the forest paradise that will do anything to stop you from leaving. I worked in a team of 9 people to develop this game using the Unity game engine and we are releasing the game on Steam later this month!

My Role:

As well as acting as the programming producer, my main focus for this project was coding the AI for all of the enemies which I implemented using behavior trees and context-based steering. Through working with our lead combat designer and character artist, I ended up creating 4 enemies in total, all with their own unique behavior and attacks. Read more about our enemy creation process and my other contributions to the project here.

Dracomaton

About the Project:

Dracomaton is a fast-paced rogue-like all about swapping between different movesets called "forms" to fight off enemies. The game was created by a team of 14 people in Unity and it is getting released on Steam later this summer.

My Role:

I joined this project partway through its development as a programmer which required rapid learning of the game's code structure in order to get up to speed on how the game worked. During my time on the project I collaborated with artists and designers to implement content such as items, which interacted with all parts of the game and therefore required lots of communication with the other programmers on the team.

Coma Dream

About the Project:

Coma Dream is a 3D puzzle-platformer made in Unity based on the dreams one of my teammates had while in a medically induced coma. Our team had 7 people on it and we developed the game in 2 weeks.

My Role:

I programmed the player controller using a finite state machine, the cuboid planet physics, and the behavior for the runaway collectible keys.

TacoCat

About the Project:

TacoCat is a 2D narrative driven game made up of truck driving and taco making minigames. I worked on this game as a part of the Game Design and Art Collaboration club which runs a mock studio ever year where around 50 students work together to develop a game.

My Role:

Soaring Hearts

About the Project:

Soaring Hearts is a 2 player co-op puzzle-platformer made in Unity based on the prompt "Hidden Tutorials". Our team consisted of 9 people and we developed the game in 2 weeks.

My Role:

For this project I collaborated with a teammate to create the 2D player controller utilizing a finite state machine and implemented all of the player animations/sounds.
